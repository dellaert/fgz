\subsection*{Toy SLAM Elimination Sequence}
This sequence approximates the variable-elimination figure from the booklet using five separate fgz files. The early panels mix factor-graph structure with directed node dependencies by letting factor-graph variables act as parents of Bayes-net nodes. As elimination proceeds, the remaining structure transitions from mixed graph fragments to a pure Bayes net.

\begin{figure}[p]
\centering
\subcaptionbox{(a)\label{fig:booklet-a}}[0.45\textwidth]{\input{booklet-a.fgz.tex}}
\hfill
\subcaptionbox{(b)\label{fig:booklet-b}}[0.45\textwidth]{\input{booklet-b.fgz.tex}}

\medskip

\subcaptionbox{(c)\label{fig:booklet-c}}[0.45\textwidth]{\input{booklet-c.fgz.tex}}
\hfill
\subcaptionbox{(d)\label{fig:booklet-d}}[0.45\textwidth]{\input{booklet-d.fgz.tex}}

\medskip

\subcaptionbox{(e)\label{fig:booklet-e}}[0.45\textwidth]{\input{booklet-e.fgz.tex}}
\caption{A five-panel elimination sequence recreated as separate fgz sources and assembled with LaTeX subfigures.}
\end{figure}

\paragraph{(a)} The starting panel mixes unary and binary factors over $x_1$, $x_2$, and $x_3$ with directed landmark nodes $l_1$ and $l_2$ above them.
\paragraph{(b)} The second panel keeps the mixed representation but shifts the unary structure to emphasize the pending elimination of $x_1$.
\paragraph{(c)} The third panel replaces $x_1$ with a directed dependency $x_2 \rightarrow x_1$ while $x_2$ and $x_3$ still carry the remaining factor-graph terms.
\paragraph{(d)} The fourth panel advances the elimination so that $x_2$ is also represented as a node, leaving only the unary terms attached to $x_3$.
\paragraph{(e)} The final panel is a pure Bayes net with the chain $x_3 \rightarrow x_2 \rightarrow x_1$, plus landmark conditionals on $l_1$ and $l_2$.
